Frequently Asked Questions


1. I think my child might need extra help. How does the Washington University Nursery School staff ensure my child receives the support they need?


If one or more children need help in a specific area, the curriculum may be adapted in a variety of ways, including one on one time with the teacher and sensory exploration. Read more about our approach to individualized support in the Curriculum Overview


2. I want to stay updated on my child's growth and learning. Are there parent teacher conferences or other forms of communication offered by the Washington University Nursery School?


The school has two Parent Conferences during the school year, one in the Fall and one in the Spring. Additional conferences may be scheduled as needed.

Communication is delivered in multiple formats throughout the year. You can stay updated on the school's events and activities through the school's website, newsletters, and emails.

To learn more about how we communicate with parents, please refer to the Parent-Staff Communication and Assessment and Parent-Teacher Conferences sections of the Parent Handbook


How is discipline incorporated into the Washington University Nursery School?


The staff and director at WUNS work to maintain a very positive and nurturing environment for each child. Classroom limits are necessary to provide a sense of order and allow teachers to spend as much time as possible providing a stimulating environment for each child. The staff is trained to anticipate potentially problematic situations; they remind children of appropriate behavior and redirect or intervene when necessary. Read more about our philosophy on discpline in the Parent Handbook
